# Business Case Toolkit

A document package to help you justify HIL investment to your organization.
The materials here are meant to be **adapted** — copy them, plug in your
numbers, and walk into your next budget conversation prepared.

## What's inside

| Folder | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| [`npv-calculator/`](npv-calculator/) | Net Present Value model for a HIL capital purchase. Input your costs, savings, and discount rate; it returns NPV, IRR, and payback period. |
| [`roi-framework/`](roi-framework/) | A structured framework for capturing direct and indirect ROI from HIL — defect escape reduction, regression cycle time, engineer time recovered, and more. |
| [`value-proposition/`](value-proposition/) | A fill-in-the-blanks value-proposition template you can tailor to your product line and audience (engineering leadership, finance, executive sponsor). |
| [`supporting-materials/`](supporting-materials/) | Reference data, citations, and worked examples that back up the numbers in the templates above. |

## Suggested order

1. Start with **value-proposition** to get clear on *why* before *how much*.
2. Use the **ROI framework** to enumerate every category of benefit honestly —
   including the soft ones.
3. Plug the quantifiable benefits into the **NPV calculator** to produce a
   single number leadership can react to.
4. Pull from **supporting-materials** when someone asks "where did that
   number come from?"

## A word on numbers

These templates are scaffolds, not prescriptions. The defaults are
illustrative — your organization's cost of capital, defect cost, and
engineering rates are what make the model meaningful. Treat the templates
as a starting structure and replace every placeholder before presenting.
